>> Tournament Formats
FORMAT #01
2-Headed Giant
Ruleset: Swedish Old School + Premodern
Structure: Swiss — Best of One Game

Ready to go completely bananas? Two-Headed Giant is a blast — and this time, Old School and Premodern unite for a format mash-up like you've never seen. Pair up with a friend and take on the field together.

FORMAT #02
Old School
Ruleset: Swedish B&R — Revenna Reprint
Structure: Swiss + Top 8

Reprints? Of course! We want everyone who loves the format to be able to play. And if you're rocking a 100% Swedish legal & UNPOWERED deck, you'll start with a bonus. Dig deep into your collection and brew.

FORMAT #03
Scryings
Ruleset: Scryings
Structure: Swiss + Top 8

A fan-crafted 116-card expansion — a reverse Chronicles that reaches forward in time to cherry-pick cards from Fallen Empires through Weatherlight. Fresh archetypes, new life for mid-tier strategies, and the silver-age soul of 93/94 kept fully intact.

>> Special Awards

Some victories aren't measured on the scoreboard — they're measured in stories, chaos, and unforgettable plays. Special Awards will be up for grabs all weekend: team glory in Battle of the Clubs, loads of prizes in our custom Old School Bingo, infinite glory for those who dare challenge time itself in Dominus Temporis, and the power-hungry few who complete the tron and claim Urza Assembled. And for the truly fearless brewers: bring the weird tech and the rare cards for your shot to become the Spice Lord.

Battle of the Clubs
Every club brings their best. Points accumulate from all members' results across every format and every round. The club with the highest average score at the end of the weekend claims the trophy — and the right to boast for the next year. Come as individuals. Compete as a team.
Dominus Temporis
Time Elemental in the main deck. No sideboard tricks, no last-minute swaps — it has to be there from game one. The Dominus Temporis title goes to the player who commits to the bit and finishes with the best overall placement. Respect the clock. Respect the 0/2.
Urza Assembled
Urza's Mine. Urza's Tower. Urza's Power Plant. Assemble all three during play and you've completed the tron — unlimited mana, unlimited ambition. This award belongs to those who pull off the circuit and prove that Urza's legacy is very much alive.
Old School Bingo
A custom bingo card packed with Old School moments. Cast a Black Lotus. Win via Time Walk. Have your Chaos Orb flip miss. Experience the chaos of a Shahrazad. Check off enough squares across the weekend and prizes are waiting — bingo was never this dangerous.
Spice Lord
Forget the tier lists. The Spice Lord belongs to the player with the most creative, unexpected, and gloriously weird deck. The more eyebrows raised across the table, the better your chances. Rare cards, unusual tech, brews nobody saw coming — this is your moment.

[ Getting There ]
Metro

From Stockholm Central Station: take the red line towards either Norsborg or Fruängen. Travel time to Hornstull is about 8 minutes, followed by a 5-10 minute walk to the venue.

Bus

Several bus lines stop near Hornstull and Långholmen, including:

Bus 4: towards Södermalm

Bus 55: towards Liljeholmen

Bus 66: towards Alvik

Get off at Hornstull or a nearby stop and walk the final 5–10 minutes to the venue.

>> Playing for a Cause

All proceeds from the event goes directly to research.

TMIOT 2027 donates all proceeds to researchers working to understand and defeat Systemisk skleros (sklerodermi) — a rare and serious autoimmune disease in which the body's own immune system attacks its connective tissue, causing the skin and internal organs to harden and scar. It can affect the lungs, heart, kidneys, and digestive system, and there is currently no healing salve for it.

Research is the only way forward. By showing up, slinging the cards you love, and competing for glory — you are directly funding the people working to change that.

>> Stockholm — While You're in Town

One of the world's most beautiful cities — built across 14 islands where Lake Mälaren meets the Baltic Sea.

Make the most of your visit:

The Power-9
  1. Gamla Stan — The medieval old town. Cobbled streets, colourful buildings, and the Royal Palace. A must.
  2. Vasa Museum — A 17th-century warship that sank on its maiden voyage and was raised intact 333 years later. Jaw-dropping.
  3. Fotografiska — World-class photography museum with a rooftop bar and stunning harbour views.
  4. ABBA The Museum — Silly? Yes. Brilliant? Also yes. Djurgården island, next door to Vasa.
  5. Södermalm — The neighbourhood where we're playing. Gritty, creative, full of great cafés and bars.
  6. Café Saturnus — Arguably Stockholm's best kanelbulle. The cinnamon buns are the size of your head.
  7. Skinnarviksberget — A short walk from the venue. The best free panoramic view of the city.
  8. Archipelago day trip — Hop on a ferry and explore the 30,000-island archipelago just outside the city.
  9. Paradox Museum — Floors full of optical illusions, impossible perspectives, and mind-bending rooms. Equal parts confusing and delightful — much like a Chains of Mephistopheles.
